"Gordon College in Olongapo, Philippines, has a rich history deeply intertwined with the socio-political landscape of the region. "

  • Founding: Gordon College traces its roots back to the aftermath of World War II when Olongapo was still under American control. In 1949, it was established as the Olongapo City Junior College, primarily to provide education for the children of American servicemen stationed at the nearby U.S. Naval Base Subic Bay.


  • Name Change: In 1952, the college was renamed to Ramon Magsaysay Memorial Junior College in honor of the popular Filipino President Ramon Magsaysay, who died in a plane crash that year. This name change reflected the college's growing connection to the Filipino community.


  • Expansion and Evolution: Over the decades, the institution evolved and expanded its academic offerings, transitioning into a full-fledged tertiary educational institution. It broadened its scope beyond serving only the children of American military personnel to cater to the wider community of Olongapo and its neighboring areas.


  • Transition to Gordon College: In 1998, the college was renamed once again to Gordon College, in honor of James L. Gordon, the father of Richard Gordon, a former mayor of Olongapo City. James Gordon was known for his contributions to the development of Olongapo during his tenure as the city mayor.


  • Academic Excellence and Community Engagement: Gordon College has since become known for its commitment to academic excellence and community engagement. It offers a wide range of undergraduate and graduate programs across various disciplines, including business, education, engineering, and liberal arts.


  • Partnerships and Collaborations: The college has forged partnerships with local and international educational institutions, as well as with government and non-governmental organizations, to enhance its academic offerings, research capabilities, and community outreach initiatives.


  • Adaptation and Resilience: Like many educational institutions, Gordon College has faced various challenges over the years, including changes in leadership, economic fluctuations, and the impact of natural disasters. However, it has demonstrated resilience and adaptability in navigating these challenges while remaining committed to its mission of providing quality education and serving the needs of its community.
