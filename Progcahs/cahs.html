Bachelor of Science in Nursing

  • This program prepares students for careers as professional nurses, focusing on patient care, health promotion, and disease prevention. The curriculum includes courses in anatomy, physiology, pharmacology, and clinical practice in various healthcare settings. Graduates are eligible to take the licensure examination for nurses and can work in hospitals, clinics, and community health organizations.


Bachelor of Science in Midwifery

  • This program educates students in the skills and knowledge needed to provide comprehensive care to women during pregnancy, childbirth, and the postpartum period. It covers topics such as maternal and newborn health, obstetrics, and reproductive health. Graduates can become licensed midwives, working in hospitals, birthing centers, and community health settings.


Graduate in Midwifery

  • This diploma program is designed for individuals who have completed a basic midwifery program and seek advanced training and specialization. It enhances the skills of practicing midwives in areas such as advanced maternal care, neonatal care, and family planning. Graduates often pursue leadership roles in midwifery, education, and healthcare administration.
